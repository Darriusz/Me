This traditional “filler-text” is supposed to have been utilised originally by a 16th cent. printer (anonymous to us), who concocted a mumble-Latin, presumably to make his reader focus on types rather than the meaning of the text. Nevertherless it is a chopped up excerpt from an actual Roman treaty – “De finibus bonorum et malorum” by Cicero, which reads properly: Neque porro quisquam est, qui dolorem ipsum, quia dolor sit, amet, consectetur, adipisci velit, sed quia non numquam eius modi tempora incidunt, ut labore et dolore magnam aliquam quaerat voluptatem.

Which is to say: “Nor again is there anyone who loves or pursues or desires to obtain pain of itself, because it is pain, but because occasionally circumstances occur in which toil and pain can procure him some great pleasure” (Loeb Classical Library, 1931). The statement which since 45BC works fine indeed to sum up most what we ever do.
