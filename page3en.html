Books

I’ve been busy translating and line-editing books for a few decades now. It is a task comparable to solving puzzles: after grasping the author’s semantic intention one has to find phrases that are going to render it in another language while simultaneously fit the preceding ones and the following ones, stay within the constraints of a certain intellectual convention and expressiveness. It’s hard to do such intensive processing of more than a few pages a day with the necessary focus therefore the job is only for the patient—the book usually boasts three hundred or more of the said pages. At the same time there are not many as rewarding experiences as getting into the mind of a knowledgeable author, navigating his mental processes, probing his personality. Quiet, peace and complete refuge from hic et nunc.

Actually it is a scientific fact that staying in the “here and now” is hugely overrated. An exceedingly sharp awareness of the objective reality takes away from our motivation and damps one’s mood. Hope (taking a mental step beyond the current reality) belongs to the theological virtues for a reason. As a result we have evolved imagination—and as a further result we may enjoy books now…

Words

I’m also tempted to mention the way general standards fall in this world - meaning mostly my country, though, as I’m going to speak about books in Poland. One of these falls being a hyphen I put in the previous sentence in accordance with the common computer-induced practice instead of typing an elegant dash (—), but there are more serious things too, like the culture of speaking, the culture of being, the culture of aspiring beautifully. A social scientist or historian might raise no objection since ironically this fall is of noble birth: it is a byproduct of democratizing of what used to belong to the intellectual elite only. Since today everyone can blog, tweet and FB-status to the whole nation or planet, we are flooded with a hurried non-quality of thought and form. We are flooded with our collective self. And the common praxis becomes readily accepted as norm.

What is expected today from someone in the business of book translating in Poland? It’s enough to have a look at a few books even by prominent publishers to find out that the answer is: not much. Now and again one encounters awkwardness of style, twisted sentences, paragraphs that fall apart, the lost logic of the reasoning. In the mythic Golden Age a book was supposed to educate, to guide towards higher culture. Nowadays it is often the book that needs to be mentally straightened by the reader so as to guess at its sensible meaning.

And the sad thing is next to nobody minds it. ”Non-literary” books (general non-fiction, popular science, popular psychology) suffer the most dramatic disaster. When challenged about the lack of stylization in quoted spoken statements, a certain translator told me shamelessly that can’t I see it’s a just a pop psychology, not literature?

Well, there are no non-literary books. Each translation should be of a literary quality, that is to say as fine and as elegant as possible. The list of my translations is dominated by “user” texts, like over a dozen strength training textbooks, but never did it occur to me to treat them in a different way than a Joyce or Homer. At times this even proves to be a greater challenge when you strive to dig a diamond hard to spot, however the worst case is when the diamonds actually are there but a superficial, off-hand attitude prevents the translator from breaking through to them. It is my satisfaction I at least have always made the effort.
